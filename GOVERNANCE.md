# Project Governance

## Principles

Tranquility is maintained openly. Decisions are guided by correctness,
standards traceability, clean-room integrity, deterministic behavior,
maintainability, security, operational safety, and the project's documented
requirements.

Convenience does not override these responsibilities. Wire compatibility,
command authorization, protocol correctness, audit integrity, and requirement
traceability are system contracts rather than optional implementation details.

## Roles

### Contributors

Anyone who submits an issue, requirement analysis, design, documentation, code,
test, review, or other project improvement is a contributor. Contributions
must follow [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) and the
[Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

### Maintainers

Maintainers triage issues, review and merge changes, manage releases, moderate
project spaces, steward security reports, and protect clean-room and
traceability boundaries.

The repository owner,
[Quinntyne Brown](https://github.com/QuinntyneBrown), is the current lead
maintainer.

## Decision making

Day-to-day decisions are made through issues and pull requests. Maintainers seek
rough consensus using requirements, published standards, acceptance tests,
benchmark evidence, and architecture decisions. The lead maintainer makes the
final repository decision when consensus cannot be reached.

Significant technical or product decisions should be recorded under
[docs/adr](docs/adr/README.md). Security-sensitive decisions may remain private.

## Becoming a maintainer

Maintainers may invite contributors who demonstrate sustained constructive
participation, sound technical judgment, reliable reviews, and consistent
adherence to project values.

Maintainer access may be removed for inactivity, security reasons, or policy
violations after reasonable notice when circumstances permit.

## Governance changes

Governance changes are proposed and reviewed through a pull request. Material
changes should remain open long enough for active contributors to respond.
